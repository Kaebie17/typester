There is no error feedback while you type (as in the real exam) — the passage only underlines the current word in blue. After the test, open Review mistakes for a side-by-side comparison of the passage and what you typed, with wrong words marked. Scoring is word-by-word. TypingMaster mode (default) — the software JKSSB reportedly uses — counts every mismatched word as one full error (a wrong letter, a capital, or a spacing slip each spoil the whole word); Net WPM = Gross − (wrong words ÷ minutes). Full/Half mode is the SSC/UPSSSC convention where capitalization, punctuation, transposition and spacing count as ½ mistakes. The exact internal method JKSSB uses isn't officially published, so both are provided as close estimates — pick whichever your coaching material describes. Gross = (keystrokes ÷ 5) ÷ minutes; test is ~10 minutes; accuracy below 90% disqualifies; backspace only fixes the current word and carries no direct penalty. Marks = (net ÷ target top speed) × 20. If you enter the exam benchmark (the real highest net speed reported in the ongoing exam), the result also shows marks against that real figure — the closest estimate to your actual batch-relative score.
